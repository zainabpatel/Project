History of BLM:

The Black Lives Matter (BLM) movement was created in 2013 by Alicia Garza, Patrisse Cullors and Opal Tometi, three Black women, in response to the acquittal of Trayvon Martin’s murderer, George Zimmerman. It is inclusive of all Black people and particularly aims to amplify the voices of women and queer people.

The movement originated in the US but has existed for many centuries in many forms and countries. It exists today in the form of protest for freedom and equality against a society that has continued to systemically deny the rights of Black people. The first protests were nearly 400 years ago, when the first African enslaved people resisted being property of white colonisers in the US.

The Emancipation Proclamation of 1862 and The Civil Rights Act of 1964 were huge cornerstones in the movement, but Black Americans still face an overwhelming amount of racism which requires overhauls of entire institutions to dismantle.

A Brief Timeline of events:

1619-1865

Harriet Tubman, a famous abolitionist, escaped slavery and helped to free over 70 slaves through the Underground Railroad. Many other enslaved people fought for their freedom.

1920s

Huge cultural changes challenged racism in Harlem, New York.

1930s

The rise of the Civil Rights movement, led by prominent figures such as Malcolm X.

1955-56:

Civil Rights protests against segregation became more widespread, particularly after Rosa Parks was arrested for refusing to give up her seat on a bus for a white man.

1963:

Martin Luther King gave his famous ‘I Have a Dream’ speech at a massive protest march in Washington, DC, calling for civil and economic rights for Black Americans.

1965:

Protest marches from Selma to Montgomery were met with violence and police resistance. Week-long protests against police brutality and racial profiling in Los Angeles resulted in the death of 3 dozen people.
